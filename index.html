Beer Type 101

Ale: Oldest style of beer. Crafted via warm-termperature fermentation for a short period of time. Sweet, full-bodied and fruity.
Lager: Fermented at a low temperature for a long time. Common among European countries. Light, crisp taste that is mellow and smooth.
Stout: Dark and roasty. Taste less sweet than porters, and often bitter, which comes from unmalted roasted barley.
Porter: Known for its dark black color and roasted malt aroma and notes. Fruity and dry in flavor.
